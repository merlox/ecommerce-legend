The top 25 ecommerce web design inspiration ideas for your store

by Merunas Grincalaitis | Apr 13, 2020 | Articles | 0 comments

“Website design inspiration ecommerce”

“Never judge a book by its cover”… Greatest lie ever told. It only takes us humans seven seconds to generate an opinion about something or someone, and this also applies to brands.

Thanks to the internet the proliferation of small brands is rising; now competition is globalized and arduous. Therefore, great questions emerge… How do I stand out amongst my competitors? How do I make a more creative website design? How can I enhance my e-commerce design?

In this post we will bring you the best website design inspiration ecommerce not only on aesthetic terms, but also:

  • Analyzing and bringing you the most creative website design inspiration in UI
  • Building a great User Experience
  • Brand identity & Content Tips

All this with 20 great cases of study from the best ecommerce design.

  1. UI Design… the juicy part

Here we will show you the most creative website design inspiration. We highlight these designs not because they are simply aesthetically pleasing, but because there’s an analysis process behind them. We highlight the top 10 UI designs based on design tendencies of 2020.

Two things to point out about UI design in 2020:

  • Empower yourself with the possibilities that technology can offer you

BUT!

  • Keep it simple… remember what architect Ludwig Mies van der Rohe once said: “Less is more”.

Here you have the top 10 design trends in 10 case of study of ecommerce design:

  1. Take advantage of the whitespace

That it receives the name of whitespace doesn’t mean it’s a white space. Whitespace is the area between the elements on a website (images, typography, icons…).

It balances elements appropriately creating a natural flow for the user to navigate through the content. Thus, making the information easier to digest and letting de website breathe.

Here we give you a great example:

  • Apple

The good use of whitespace focalizes the attention of the users and it’s a good tool for CTA. As well, it shows in a pretty spectacular way all the products.

https://www.apple.com/

  1. Enhance the Dark Mode Design

Design an ecommerce with color tones predominantly dark or that have a dark mode enabled, allowing an interface switch. It’s an awesome ecommerce design, as it transmits elegance and elements in clear or strong tonalities over dark tones achieve that the elements outstand intensely. Here we give two astonishing website design inspirations ecommerce.

http://www.formigari.it/

https://canneseries.com/en/

  1. Play with the typographies

Endow your typographies with some type of interaction, use different typographies or play with them. Turn them around or alternate the base line typography.

You can even make them overlap with other elements.

  1. Use creative illustrations

Creative illustrations are a huge ally to approach customers in a more enjoyable way. They are also great tools for storytelling.

https://pitch-tents.co.uk/

These types of illustrations are outstanding. This is a huge web design trend of 2020. They truly merge in a way that seems more natural. A great rule-breaking trend that is really dominating this year.

2. Building a great User Experience

User experience is a key factor in ecommerce. Not paying attention to this has a deep negative impact in sales and other KPI. Consider that the optimal number of clicks to acquire the main product is 3.

If loading time is very long, the user will leave. 4 out of 10 users leave the website if this happens.

If it’s not aesthetic and simple they will abandon the website. First impressions are owned to this with 94%, which is linked to the confidence and credibility, as 75% of times it’s determinant for the purchase.

Here we have 5 great website design inspiration ecommerce.

  1. PayPal

From PayPal we point out a simple usability, straight to the point and minimalist design which allows a fluid UX. Sending or receiving funds, as well as making other functions usually requires 2 to 3 clicks. If it wouldn’t be this way, users would abandon the site. EVERYTHING matters when talking about UX: The color palette for example, chosen with great precision to show the CTAs in an adequate way, easing the identification and interpretation for the brain, counting with short, simple and intuitive information modules and showing functionalities in other platforms witch interactive functions.

  1. ASOS

ASOS is a grate case of study and therefore an appropriate website design inspiration ecommerce in terms of both UI and UX. However, we´re going to analyze this brand as an UX example of fashion ecommerce website design.

First, “ethical” brands which reward the user for their purchases and consumption, will be highly rewarded. “Fast brands” such as Zara, H&M or Forever21 have had 6% less visitors in relation to last year. This is due to the great advantages offered by small ecommerce’s.

Let’s see how:

  • They reward you upon arrival. 30, 50 and 70$ discount in 1 click.

This invites the user to continue navigating, search products and purchase them.

  • New season products in 1 click.

Just below the discount code we can find products from the new season that are just one 1 click away.

We must point out the hover state in the “shop now” button. The color changes in the buttons are essential. This way the user knows that it’s a button and that it is operational.

  • Surprise, surprise… branded content!

As you can see, ecommerce giants exploit the branded content linked to its product generating engagement and community.

  • Extended return days!

The confidence brought by the possibility of returning a product has no limits, therefore guarantying a purchase unconditionally.

  • Videos showing the product. Multimedia content captivates.

Shopping online can be risky for many consumers. Therefore it is important to show the product the best way possible. ASOS performs a combination of images and videos tremendously adequate, aesthetic and minimalist.

  • ASOS’s superstar. Fit Assistant Size.

Since shopping clothes online may be very tricky as we can’t try out the garments, ASOS developed the Fit Assistant Size.

An excellent function to fight the doubts and insecurities of shopping clothes online. Moreover, combined with return policies, it’s a trust indicator that drastically enhances UX.

  1. Patagonia

Another exceptional fashion ecommerce website design and you will see why.

  • Products perfectly identified and categorized. It only takes you 3 clicks to make a purchase.

Moreover, products are exposed in the form of a carrousel, which makes it easy, fast and intuitive. You can appreciate products in a faster way but also more detailed as you can only see 4 products per slide, which makes it possible to pay attention to each product without none of them going unnoticed. On the other hand, they have a legend for the slides you make, making it easier to go back to previous pages.

  • Tons of product information

Remember that information in ecommerce is security, and security is translated into sales.

  • Never stop dreaming selling… Upsells & Cross sells

An excellent upsell (showing related products of higher value) and cross sell (selling you complementary products to the one you are purchasing) strategy is developed. Besides, with a great UX, showing a variety of colors.

  1. Duolingo

Duolingo is a great website design inspiration ecommerce due to its excellent CTR. Learning can be a long, tedious and complicated process, even more when we are talking about a language. Duolingo is aware of this and has designed a simple webpage where you don’t even need to make a single scroll to start learning. In a little more than 3 clicks you have started to learn a language.

Notice two key aspects that incredibly enhance the UX of Duolingo. The greatest concern of any ecommerce is that users abandon their websites.

  • Realize that Duolingo gives you the option of skipping the registration step. A tedious step for many and that is a reason of leaving an ecommerce.

A UX without complications, again straight to the point, which invites you to consume its service straight away. Consider the UX in literally every single step. No matter how good your product is, if your website has a boring and complex UX, consumers will abandon for sure.

  • Simplicity as a norm. You must know your product. Drive and focus user’s attention in the areas that needs to be done.
  1. TopShop

Topshop is for sure a great website design inspiration ecommerce. It’s a fashion ecommerce with a HUGE catalogue. With MANY things. What does this promote? That users leave on the first chance. Too many options, categories, subpages, etc… TopShop fought against this issue with a UX that fosters the visualization of the content. You don’t even have to click in order to know what awaits you. Just by hovering the cursor you can see what you will find in every category. And prices… prices everywhere. From the beginning you can see 4 USP (Unique Selling Proposition) which invite to consume.

  • 30%OFF
  • Free standard delivery on all orders
  • Extended returns
  • Premier delivery with discount

Another outstanding element is the preview without having to exit the page where you were, making it easier for the user to know if he likes a product or not and the purchase process. This way you assure he will continue to navigate in your ecommerce. Just one click to interact. No long steps.

It is very important to study the UX of your ecommerce in every page and subpage. If something goes wrong, even a single thing, the most probable outcome is that they will abandon your ecommerce. Thus, analyze, optimize y try to make it as intuitive and interactive as possible.

3. Brand identity & content tips

One of the most important things of any ecommerce. Efficiency of any creative website design resides in 2 great pillars: having a solid corporate identity and developing a good content strategy that is aligned with that identity. This implies 3 elements which are key to building a brand:

  • Having a set of corporate colors and respecting them
  • Having a typography and respecting it
  • Having a voice and communication line and respecting it

The difficult thing is not so much having those elements but respecting them in time and with actions. Identity is everything, much more in the world of ecommerce as we only have online channels to meet with our users. Many brands end up doing pretty things (or not) that do not bring:

  • Homogeneity
  • Nor identity
  • And therefore, generates confusion within the consumer.

So:

  • Organize yourself: Define who you are.
  • Who are you looking forward to serving, and what is the purpose of your product? Consider all these variables. You must define these questions to communicate and sell in an appropriate way.

Once you have, it is essential to generate a good branded content, which corresponds with your values and philosophy and above all… be constant. Lack of communication is a death sentence. Thus, don’t stop communicating. Have a presence in social media, send newsletters, generate your own contents in a blog… possibilities are infinite and, executed in a creative way, these are truly attractive.

Here are some case studies:

  1. Airbnb. Airbnb magazine.

Airbnb preaches unique experiences, unforgettable moments, closeness and the discovery of the most mysterious and recondite places which you wouldn’t discover in traditional tourism, thanks to connivance and contact with local population, which in many cases even become touristic guides and friends. Now, how do you convert that in a truly attractive content without making it played-out? Airbnb created Airbnb Magazine.

An online magazine with a great variety of articles, where even consumers participate. This way they enhance the value of guests and excellently generate a great content, creating an exceptional word of mouth. But don’t be discouraged, just because your ecommerce is not of the magnitude of Airbnb’s (yet), it doesn’t mean that you can’t do something truly creative. If, for example, you have an ecommerce of online training in stock markets, perhaps you could write a weekly (or even with greater frequency) bulletin with the most relevant economic and financial news, as well as tips on how to invest, setting base on those news. Producing this way useful, consistent and branded content.

  1. Zara. Living Zara

Zara has a great fashion ecommerce website design that in combination with a branded content strategy has achieved a great positioning and increase in its sales.

Living Zara was a campaign by Zara by the hand of retro, alternative and bohemian influencers with which it managed to bring closer the brand to its consumers. Thanks to the account @livingzara and its hashtag #livingzara, not only you were displayed with seasonal garments from the hand of influencers but also from consumers.

This way, the brand achieved a great positioning, linking products and publications to their ecommerce, making them easier to find and buy.

To this end, try to get in contact with influencers or public figures that can act as prescribers of your brand. The trust they bring and the easy they make showing your product, will generate traffic for your ecommerce, which will end up being tremendously useful.

  1. Coca Cola. Coca Cola Journey.

Coca Cola created Coca Cola Journey, where they publish and share content related to their history, making an emphasis in their values. In a different and interactive way, with mobile panels and multimedia content. This is a discrete immersion in the brand and its products.

  1. Adidas. Mygirls.

This is a branded content strategy targeted to teenagers and young women. A space destined to set the consumer in the epicenter of the brand, bringing the brand closer to the consumer. Their blog is their star. Totally oriented to transmitting overcoming stories, making the costumers enjoy the storytelling and managing to empathize and feel identified with these stories and the brand. All this without leaving aside the sales, as it brings ideas about products one can purchase.
